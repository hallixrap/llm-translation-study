Vaccine: What You Need to Know Please visit www.immunize.org/vis 1. Why get vaccinated? 2. Recombinant shingles vaccine Recombinant shingles vaccine can prevent shingles. Recombinant shingles vaccine can provide strong protection for preventing shingles. By preventing shingles, recombinant shingles vaccine can also prevent PHN and other complications. Shingles (also called shingles, or simply shingles) is a painful rash, usually with blisters. In addition to rash, shingles can also cause fever, headache, chills or stomach upset. In very rare cases, shingles may cause complications such as pneumonia, hearing problems, blindness, encephalitis (encephalitis) or death. Recombinant shingles vaccine is recommended for:  Adults 50 years and older  Adults 19 years and older with weakened immune systems due to illness or treatment The risk of getting shingles increases with age. The most common complication of shingles is long-term nerve pain called postherpetic neuralgia (PHN). PHN occurs where the shingles rash appears and may last for months or years after the rash disappears. PHN pain can be severe and disabling. The shingles vaccine is given in two doses.
